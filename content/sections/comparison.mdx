Traditional political risk insurance was built for project finance in the 1990s. It excludes the very markets now contested by Beijing and leaves U.S. firms uncovered when authoritarian regimes weaponize bureaucracy. TPRI changes the equation with umbrella policies, mandatory recovery, and strategic zone compacts that keep allies aligned.
